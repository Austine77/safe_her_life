Know your rights

Read your rights before you report

SafeHerLife is built to help users share reports more safely. This page explains important privacy, safety, and support rights in a clear and professional way.

1. You can report safely You can share a report using only the details you are comfortable providing. Anonymous reporting remains available unless you choose to add email or phone contact.
2. You control what you share You may submit text, a voice note, or both. Avoid adding private details that could put you at risk unless they are necessary for support staff to help.
3. Your case ID matters After a report is submitted, save your case ID. It lets you track updates and message staff later without exposing your identity publicly.
4. Your privacy should be respected Only authorized staff should review case details and secure chat messages linked to your case. Use the platform only from a device and location that feels safe.
5. Emergency danger needs urgent help If you are in immediate danger, move to a safe place and contact emergency services, local authorities, or a trusted person nearby as quickly as possible.

Safety reminders

  • Use quick exit or close the page if someone is watching your screen.
  • Store your case ID in a private place you can access later.
  • Use secure chat only after loading a valid case.
  • Choose anonymous mode if direct contact may expose you.
